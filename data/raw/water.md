# Finding water and making it safe to drink

After breathable air, water is the most important resource in an emergency. A
person can survive only a few days without water. This text is a self-written
sample document for the Survival101 RAG project and is not a substitute for
professional training.

## Finding water sources

Running water from streams and rivers is preferable to standing water, because
standing water collects more germs and algae. Rainwater is usually very clean
and can be collected directly, for example with a tarp or large leaves. Morning
dew can be wiped off grass with a cloth and wrung out. You more often find water
in valleys, at the base of rocks, and where the vegetation is especially green.

## Why water must be treated

Even clear water can contain pathogens: bacteria, viruses and parasites such as
giardia. These cause diarrhea and vomiting, which dangerously accelerates fluid
loss in an emergency. Water from nature should therefore always be treated
before you drink it.

## Method 1: Boiling

Boiling is the most reliable method to kill pathogens. Bring the water to a
rolling boil and keep it boiling for at least one minute. At high altitude above
2000 meters, make it three minutes, because water boils at a lower temperature
there. Boiling does not remove chemical contaminants or turbidity.

## Method 2: Filtering

A filter removes suspended particles and many pathogens. A simple emergency
filter can be built from a bottle filled with layers of sand, charcoal and small
stones. Important: a self-made filter makes water clearer but does not reliably
kill all germs. After filtering, you should also boil the water.

## Method 3: Chemical treatment

Water purification tablets or a few drops of unscented chlorine bleach kill
germs. After adding them, the water must sit for about 30 minutes. This method is
practical because it is light and compact, but it slightly changes the taste.

## Safety note

When in doubt, never drink untested water from standing puddles, or near
industry or agriculture. Chemical contamination cannot be removed by boiling.
These notes are general guidance and are not a substitute for solid survival
training.
